Renewable Energy in Australia

Renewable Energy Transformation Over Two Decades (1998-2023)

This graph shows the cumulative production of renewable energy sources (bioenergy, hydro, solar, and wind) from 1998 to 2023. Each source of renewable energy is represented by a different color stacked on top of each other, showing the total energy production for each year.

In this graph, we can see that solar and wind energy production have seen the most dramatic growth since around 2010. Hydro remains relatively stable, while bioenergy shows a slow but consistent upward trend. This also highlights the growing importance of renewable energy, with solar and wind emerging as the dominant contributors to the overall production.

Fastest Growing Renewable Energy Sources in Australia

This graph shows the annual percentage change in renewable energy projects across four energy types: hydro, wind, solar, and bioenergy, from 1998 to 2023.

The graph shows considerable fluctuations, particularly in solar and wind projects, which exhibit periods of rapid growth followed by stabilization or slower increases. Solar energy displays particularly sharp increases in some years, indicating periods of heightened investment or technological advances. Wind energy also shows notable surges, though its growth pattern is less volatile than that of solar. Hydro and bioenergy on the other hand show smaller and more consistent changes over the years.

Map of Renewable Energy Projects

This map illustrates the geographical locations of renewable energy projects across Australia, categorized by different technologies and their corresponding costs.

The other 2 graphs don't show where the growth is happening within Australia and this map fills this gap by showing the specific locations of renewable energy projects, helping to visualize where investments in renewable energy are taking place.
